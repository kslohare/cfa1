# Fixed Cost vs Variable Cost (CFA Level 1 – Core Concept)

## 📌 1. Fixed Cost

### Definition
Costs that **do NOT change** with the level of production or sales.

### Key Characteristics
- Remain constant regardless of output
- Paid even if production = 0
- Time-based (monthly, yearly)

### Examples
- Rent
- Salaries (permanent staff)
- Insurance
- Depreciation

### 💡 Analogy
Think of **house rent**:
- Whether you stay or travel, you still pay rent  
→ Same logic = Fixed Cost

---

## 📌 2. Variable Cost

### Definition
Costs that **change directly** with production or sales volume.

### Key Characteristics
- Increase with more production
- Decrease with less production
- Output-based

### Examples
- Raw materials
- Packaging
- Sales commission
- Production electricity

### 💡 Analogy
Think of **fuel in your bike**:
- More you ride → more fuel used  
→ That’s Variable Cost

---

## 📊 Key Differences

| Feature | Fixed Cost | Variable Cost |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Changes with production | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| Exists at zero production | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| Nature | Time-based | Activity-based |
| Example | Rent | Raw material |

---

## 📈 Why This Matters in Finance (CFA Insight)

### 1. Profit Behavior
Profit = Revenue − (Fixed Cost + Variable Cost)

- High fixed cost → losses at low sales
- High sales → profits increase sharply

---

### 2. Operating Leverage (Important Concept)

- Companies with **high fixed cost** have **high operating leverage**

### Meaning:
- Small increase in sales → **large increase in profit**

### 💡 Analogy
Think of a **movie theater**:
- Fixed cost = rent, staff
- Once seats fill → profit grows fast

---

## 📌 Simple Example

- Selling Price = ₹100
- Variable Cost = ₹60
- Fixed Cost = ₹10,000

### Case 1: Sell 100 units
Revenue = 10,000  
Variable Cost = 6,000  
Profit = 10,000 − 6,000 − 10,000 = **-6,000 (Loss)**

### Case 2: Sell 500 units
Revenue = 50,000  
Variable Cost = 30,000  
Profit = 50,000 − 30,000 − 10,000 = **10,000 (Profit)**

👉 Fixed cost same, but profit improves with volume

---

## 🎯 Final Summary

- **Fixed Cost** → Stable but risky at low sales  
- **Variable Cost** → Flexible and safer  
- **High Fixed Cost + High Sales = High Profit**
